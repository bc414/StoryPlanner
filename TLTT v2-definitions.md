# Track Types

Track types define the cognitive mode and authorial perspective of a note track.

- **Ontology**: Ontology Notes (Layer 1) - written by a world builder in god-mode defining what the rules of the fictional universe are
- **Civilization**: Civilization Notes (Layer 3) - written by a world builder building things made by in-universe agents in response to their ontology
- **History**: History Notes (Layer 2) - written by an in-universe historian reporting facts
- **Characterization**: Characterization Notes (Layer 3) - written by a psychologist asserting the truth of what makes a character who they are
- **PageDesign**: Page Notes - written by a director staging what the reader observes on the page
- **WorldInference**: World Inference Notes - written by a reader psychologist designing what a reader should infer from what is on the page
- **ThematicEvidence**: Thematic Evidence Notes (Layer 5) - written by a philosopher deploying evidence for a reader to arrive at a thematic proposition
- **NotesToSelf**: Notes to self - written by the author, talking about the story planning project and process
- **Analogies**: Analogical Notes - written by the author to document real-world inspiration (can be historical or present-day inspiration)
- **NarrativeArchitecture**: Narrative Architecture Notes (Layer 4) - written by the author planning out how the reader should experience the story world
- **Canon**: Canon Notes - written by the author to constrain the story within established canon, build upon and recontextualize canon
- **Allegories**: Allegorical Notes - written by the author as a reader inference target for social commentary

---

# Subject Definitions

Subject types define the categories of entities (characters, locations, etc.) that can own notes and appear in the story planner.

- **Uncategorized**
- **Character**
- **Bond**
- **Organization**
- **World Law**
- **Civilizational System**
- **Technology**

---

# Note Track Definitions

Each note track definition describes one category of notes that can be attached to subjects. The text below are helpers for an author to write and audit notes of each type.

## Bond

### Project-Wide Tracks

#### Source Material References

Track Type: Canon

**Display Question**

What canon interactions between these characters or established fanon does TLTT build on or recontextualize?

---

#### History

Track Type: History

**Display Question**

What is the history between the two characters before the story starts?

---

#### The Spark

Track Type: Characterization

**Display Question**

Why do these two characters have something special and unique to them? There must be data for both sides of the bond.

---

#### Themes

Track Type: ThematicEvidence

**Display Question**

What themes do this bond represent?

---

#### Garden Notes

Track Type: NotesToSelf

**Display Question**

What should I take into account when planning this relationship?

---

#### Story Plan

Track Type: NarrativeArchitecture

**Display Question**

What is the plan for events about the bond's formation and evolution, in story order?

---

#### Reader Opinion

Track Type: NarrativeArchitecture

**Display Question**

What is the plan for the reader's opinion of the relationship, in story order, starting from tropes or prior assumptions?

---

#### Scene Theme Evidence

How does the bond serve as evidence for a thematic proposition?

---

All characterization should have a reveal somewhere in the project.

#### Theme Plan

Track Type: ThematicEvidence

**Display Question**

What thematic propositions does this character embody throughout their apperances, how they clash with reader prior expecations, their character development arc, or their character reader perception gap across the project?

---

#### Allegories

Track Type: Allegories

What kind of demographic does this character represent in the real world?

---

#### Garden Notes

Track Type: NotesToSelf

What story planning work still needs to be done regarding this character?

---

#### Character Appearance Plan

Track Type: NarrativeArchitecture

**Display Question**

**Usage Directive**

Fill this in after links are done.

#### Reader Opinion Plan

Track Type: NarrativeArchitecture

**Display Question**

**Usage Directive**

---

#### Character Development

**Display Question**

What is the plan for how the character changes, in reading order?

**Usage Directive**

Fill this in after links are done.

---

#### Character Reader Perception Gap Plan

Track Type: NarrativeArchitecture

**Display Question**

What is the story-wide plan for calibrating the gap between the character's earnest self-perception of themself versus the reader's understanding of the character across the character's development arc? Applies to prominent POV characters only.

### Link Tracks

#### Scene Theme Evidence

Track Type: ThematicEvidence

**Display Question**

How does the character's actions and/or existing psychology serve as evidence for a thematic proposition?

---

#### Reader Prior Belief Clash

Track Type: ThematicEvidence

**Display Question**

---

#### Character Development Meaning

Track Type: ThematicEvidence

**Display Question**

What should the reader conclude the change means, which serves as evidence for a thematic proposition?

---

#### Gap Meaning

Track Type: ThematicEvidence

**Display Question**

From the continuous gap between what the POV character earnestly believes about themself, which the reader inhabits, and what the reader understands about the character from other context, what should the reader conclude from processing that gap which serves as evidence for a thematic proposition?

---

#### Revelation

Track Type: PageDesign

**Display Question**

What history should be disclosed on the page?

---

#### Character Actions

Track Type: PageDesign

**Display Question**

What does this character do on the page? What behavior is shown of this character on the page?

---

#### Inferred Psychology

Track Type: WorldInference

**Display Question**

What should the reader infer about the character's existing psychology?

---

#### Reader Prior Belief Update

Track Type: WorldInference

**Display Question**

What would be the reader's prior opinion of the character? How do new revelations, events, or inferences on their characterization update the reader's opinion?

---

#### Inferred Character Development

Track Type: WorldInference

**Display Question**

What should the reader infer about how this character is changing?

---

#### Character-Reader Perception Gap

Track Type: WorldInference

**Display Question**

What should the reader understand about the POV character, from observing them and knowing structural context, which the POV character earnestly doesn't understand about themself?

**Usage Directive**

The POV character's earnest understanding of themself must be conveyed through Free Indirect Discourse. It should not come across as ironic or self-perceptive; it should be earnest. The reader inhabits the POV character's earnest interiority. The reader should know from other POVs and/or the wider context and their own lived experience that the POV character's understanding of themself is wrong or incomplete.

**Display Question**

---

#### Analogies

Track Type: Analogies

**Display Question**

---

#### System Ontology

Track Type: Ontology

**Display Question**

What are the working rules of the system? What does it objectively do? What purpose does it serve?

---

#### Causality of Creation

Track Type: Civilization

**Display Question**

Why did its creators create this system? What (layer 1) material conditions led to the creation of the system?

---

#### History

Track Type: History

**Display Question**

What is the history of the system's establishment, its effects on the story world, and, if applicable, the system's downfall?

---

#### Theme Plan

Track Type: ThematicEvidence

**Display Question**

What thematic propositions does the rules or effects of this system serve as evidence for?

---

#### Allegories

Track Type: Allegories

**Display Question**

What relevant truths about the way the real world works does this fictional civilizational system represent?

**Usage Directive**

Readers should be able to arrive at the allegory through inference. The allegory must not appear on the page.

---

#### Garden Notes

Track Type: NotesToSelf

**Display Question**

What should I take into account when planning this civilizational system?

---

#### Storytelling Plan

Track Type: NarrativeArchitecture

**Display Question**

What is the plan for dramatizing the establishment of the system, the system's effects on the story world, and, if applicable, the system's downfall?

---

#### Reader Opinion

Track Type: NarrativeArchitecture

**Display Question**

What is the project-wide design of the desired reader's opinion of the system, including expected starting assumptions and then updating through story order?

---

### Link Tracks

#### Scene Theme Evidence

Track Type: ThematicEvidence

**Display Question**

What does the system's rules or usage (disclosed or inferred) mean about the real world? What thematic proposition is it evidence for?

---

#### Reader Prior Belief Clash

Track Type: ThematicEvidence

**Display Question**

How does the process of updating their prior belief with the new one serve as evidence for a thematic proposition?

---

#### Revelation

Track Type: PageDesign

**Display Question**

What history or truth about how the system works or why it was created is being disclosed on the page?

---

#### Demonstration

Track Type: PageDesign

**Display Question**

How is the system having an effect on the story events on the page?

---

#### World Inference

Track Type: WorldInference

**Display Question**

What should the reader infer about how the system works, why it was created, and/or what purpose the system serves?

**Usage Directive**

These conclusions reflect real life, and thus cannot appear on the page. The reader should arrive at the conclusion themself so they internalize the meaning rather than being told.

---

#### Reader Prior Belief Update

Track Type: WorldInference

**Display Question**

What would be the reader's prior assumptions or understanding of the system? How does the disclosure or their inference update their belief?

### Link Tracks

#### Scene Theme Evidence

Track Type: ThematicEvidence

**Display Question**

What thematic proposition does the technology's usage serve as evidence for?

---

#### Reader Prior Belief Clash

Track Type: ThematicEvidence

**Display Question**

How does the process of updating their prior belief with the new one serve as evidence for a thematic proposition?

---

#### Revelation

Track Type: PageDesign

**Display Question**

What is being revealed about the technology's history of invention, history of usage, or how it works?

**Usage Directive**

These details can go on the page.

**Audit Directive**

Only aspects of the technology that are invented for the story without direct real-life parallels can go on the page.

---

#### Usage

Track Type: PageDesign

**Display Question**

What is the technology doing in this scene?

**Usage Directive**

These details specify what needs to go on the page.

---

#### World Inference

Track Type: WorldInference

**Display Question**

What should the reader infer about how the technology works or why it was invented?

**Usage Directive**

These details need inference seeds.

**Audit Directive**

Aspects of the technology that have direct real-life parallels should not go on the page and should be inferred so that readers form the implications themselves rather than being told an opinion or stance to take on the real-life equivalent.

## World Law

### Project-Wide Tracks

#### Source Material References

Track Type: Canon

**Display Question**

How does this world law connect with established canon depictions?

**Usage Directive**

Reader prior knowledge predictions should take into account source material references.

---

#### Analogies

Track Type: Analogies

**Display Question**

What real-world mechanics inspire the design of this fantasy world law?

---

#### World Truth

Track Type: Ontology

**Display Question**

What is the invariant truth of this in-universe law or world mechanic, independent of character knowledge or perception?

**Usage Directive**

Anything revealed on page should be calibrated against the truth here. If the page text contradicts the truth, it should be intentional due to character epistemological limits.

---

#### Historical Study

Track Type: History

**Display Question**

What is the history of how the world truth was studied in-universe by characters, and how the characters' understanding of the world truth evolved through in-universe history?

**Usage Directive**

What the characters say on page should not contradict the in-universe timeline of understanding and study established here. Refer to the world date.

---

What thematic propositions does the design of this world law serve as evidence for?

---

Track Type: NotesToSelf

---

#### Stakes

Track Type: NarrativeArchitecture

**Display Question**

Why does this scene matter?

---

#### Outcome

Track Type: NarrativeArchitecture

**Display Question**

What objectively changed in the story world by the end of this scene?

---

#### Delivery Blueprint

Track Type: PageDesign

**Display Question**

What should appear on the page? All subject material that is planned to be on the page should be covered.

---

What should appear on the page to lead to the desired reader inferences?

---

#### Theme Evidence

**Display Question**
